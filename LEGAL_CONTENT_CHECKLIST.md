# Legal Content and Asset Licensing Checklist

Use this checklist before each major release.

## 1. Ownership and registration
- Confirm owner name (individual or company) is consistent in footer and legal pages.
- Confirm copyright notice appears in the footer.
- Keep records of first publication date and major revisions.
- Keep git history and source files archived as authorship evidence.
- Consider formal copyright registration in your jurisdiction for stronger enforcement.

## 2. Website text and design
- Confirm original copy has a clear author trail.
- Confirm UI/layout components are your own work or licensed templates.
- Confirm no copied text blocks from third-party websites without permission.

## 3. Images, icons, and graphics
- For each image, record:
  - source URL,
  - license type,
  - attribution requirement,
  - commercial-use permission,
  - modification permission.
- Ensure attribution is added where license requires it.
- Replace any image with unclear ownership or license.

## 4. Fonts and media assets
- Confirm all web fonts are licensed for web embedding and commercial use.
- Confirm audio/video clips (if any) have distribution rights.
- Confirm no stock asset is used beyond allowed seat/project limits.

## 5. Open data and third-party materials
- Verify each external dataset or publication is cited correctly.
- Keep source links current and versioned where possible.
- Distinguish facts/data from your original analysis and commentary.
- Respect terms for redistribution when embedding data extracts.

## 6. Legal pages and notices
- Keep Terms of Use page published and linked in footer.
- Include copyright + IP clause in Terms.
- Include takedown/permissions contact email in Terms.
- Add Privacy Policy if collecting personal data, cookies, analytics, or forms.

## 7. Brand protection
- Reserve primary domain variations to reduce impersonation risk.
- If brand matters commercially, file trademark for name/logo in priority classes.
- Keep a brand usage guide for consistent authorized use.

## 8. Enforcement readiness
- Keep a dated evidence bundle of original drafts and design files.
- Prepare a standard takedown email template for infringements.
- Monitor for content scraping or unauthorized cloning.

## 9. Release gate
- No release if any critical asset lacks clear license proof.
- No release if legal contact email is missing from Terms.
- No release if footer legal links are broken.
